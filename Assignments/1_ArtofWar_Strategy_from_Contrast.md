### 1. Subdivisions better than big block of army
    - Popular:
        - the army should be as big as possible
    - Contrast:
        - Fighting with smaller subdivisions is better:
            - CHENG: facing the Enemy --> something that the enemy has his attention fixed upon --> do this in front of enemy --> PASSIVE bcoz we are waiting for opportunity
        - CHI: lateral division --> something which catches the enemy by surprise --> do these    abnormal maneuvers to win --> ACTIVE:

### 2. what we show vs how we are 
    - Popular: Show what we are && "we can only control our moves"
        - you might be confused if you look so, you might be timid if you look so, and you might be weak if you look so.
    
    - Contrast: for trapping the enemy, you need to look confused, but for that you need to have perfect discipline. Similarly, to display timidity, you must have extreme courage, and similarly, to show weakness, you should have extreme strength.
        
        - IDEA: if we are stronger or comparable in strength, then we should simulate weakness, but if we are inferior, then we should simulate strength --> **In fact, all the enemy's movements should be determined by the signs that we choose to give him**

### EXtending on 2: One of the simulations is of disorder (16)
    - Popular: we should show enemy how strong we are 
    - Contrast: split into subdivisions so that enemy thinks there is disorder in the army

    ```text 
        “16.  Amid the turmoil and tumult of battle, there may be
        seeming disorder and yet no real disorder at all; amid confusion
        and chaos, your array may be without head or tail, yet it will be
        proof against defeat.
        [Mei Yao-ch`en says:  "The subdivisions of the army having
        been previously fixed, and the various signals agreed upon,  the
        separating and joining, the dispersing and collecting which will
        take place in the course of a battle, may give the appearance of
        disorder when no real disorder is possible.  Your formation may
        be without head or tail, your dispositions all topsy-turvy,  and
        yet a rout of your forces quite out of the question."]”
    ```

### SELF RESTRAINT is necessary (13, 14)
    - Popular: Fire back immediately 
    - Contrast: wait for the turn
        -   the story of the Nelson's ship 

    ```text
        “     13.  The quality of decision is like the well-timed swoop of
        a falcon which enables it to strike and destroy its victim.
        [The Chinese here is tricky and a certain key word in the
        context it is used defies the best efforts of the translator.  Tu
        Mu defines this word as "the measurement or estimation of
        distance."  But this meaning does not quite fit the illustrative
        simile in ss. 15.  Applying this definition to the falcon,  it
    ```
